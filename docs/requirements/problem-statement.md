# Problem Statement

Traditional premium Damascene handcrafted products (especially wooden pieces inlaid with mother-of-pearl) are often presented through unstructured, generic commercial platforms that fail to reflect their cultural, artistic, and heritage value.

Most mainstream e-commerce solutions optimize for speed and mass selling, which conflicts with the calm, museum-like experience required to present heritage products as collectible art objects rather than commodities.

In addition, Syria has experienced prolonged circumstances over recent years that negatively impacted tourism and reduced both local and international demand for traditional crafts. As a result, some Damascene artisanal professions have seen declining market exposure, making them vulnerable to gradual disappearance over time. This creates a need for a professional digital intermediary that connects local Syrian artisans with broader audiences locally and globally, while preserving authenticity and elevating the presentation of the craft.

The problem addressed by this project is how to design and implement a digital platform that:
- Preserves cultural authenticity in presentation and content
- Presents products as collectible art objects, not mass-market commodities
- Connects Syrian artisans with wider markets (including international audiences)
- Scales technically without sacrificing brand identity and heritage integrity
- Contributes to sustainable local economic value by enabling a structured and premium sales channel
